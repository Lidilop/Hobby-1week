Running Tips

  • Warm Up Properly

    Always start your runs with a proper warm-up. Dynamic stretching and light jogging can help prepare your muscles and prevent injuries.

  • Listen to Your Body

    Pay attention to any discomfort or pain during your runs. Rest and seek medical advice if you experience persistent pain or signs of injury.

  • Stay Hydrated

    Drink plenty of water before, during, and after your runs, especially in hot weather, to stay properly hydrated.

  • Gradually Increase Mileage

    When increasing your running distance, do so gradually to avoid overuse injuries. Follow a structured training plan to build endurance safely.
